5. Order of Play
  5.1. Determine Player Order
  5.2. Auction Power Plants
  5.3. Buy Resources
  5.4. Build
  5.5. Bureaucracy
  5.6. Check for Game End
  5.7. Repeat Rule 5.1 Until Game End
6. Determining Player Order
  6.1. If First Turn, Randomly Assign Player Order
    6.1.1 Then Apply Rule 6.2 After First Auction Phase Finished
  6.2. First Player has Most Cities (Slot 1), Last Player has Least Cities (Last Slot)
  6.3. If a Tie, The Player With the Higher Cost Plant is First
7. Auction Power Plants
  7.1. Turn Order From First to Last (Slot 1 to Last Slot); Current Player is the Auction Leader
  7.2. Auction Leader Chooses:
    7.2.1. Pass
      7.2.1.1 Skip to Rule 7.4
    7.2.2. Start the Bid on a Plant In the Current Market
      7.2.2.1. Minimum Bid is Cost of Plant
      7.2.2.2. Initial Bid Has No Maximum
        7.2.2.2.1. Except for Max of Auction Leader's Money
      7.2.2.3. Start Bidding
        7.2.2.3.1. Go to Rule 7.3
  7.3. Bidding For Plant
    7.3.1. Turn Order is Clockwise Starting From Auction Leader; Current Player is Bidder
    7.3.2. Bidder Chooses:
      7.3.2.1. Pass, Skip to Rule 7.3.3
      7.3.2.2. Bid at Least 1 Higher on Plant
        7.3.2.2.1. Player Must Pass if They Own Less Money Than Lowest Allowable Bid
    7.3.3. Repeat Rule 7.3.2 Until Only One Bidder Has Not Passed
    7.3.4. Remaining Player Pays Last Bid Price
    7.3.5. Remove Power Plant from Current Market
    7.3.6. Give Power Plant to Remaining Bidder
    7.3.7. This Player May Not Become Auction Leader or Bid on Plants in This Auction Round
    7.3.8. Add Another Plant to Market; Refer to Rule XXX
  7.4. Repeat Rule 7.1 Until No Players Remain
  7.5. If a Power Plant Was Not Purchased in an Auction Round, Refer to Rule XXX
8. Buy Resources
  8.1. Turn Order From Last To First (Last Slot to Slot 1); Current Player is the Purchaser
  8.2. Purchaser Chooses:
    8.2.1. Pass, Skip to Rule 8.3
    8.2.2. Buy Resources
      8.2.2.1. Total Cost May Not Exceed Purchaser's Owned Money; refer to rule XXX
      8.2.2.2. Purchaser May Not Buy Resources if a Plant Can Not Store It; refer to rule XXX
      8.2.2.3. Purchaser May Not Buy More Resources Than What a Plant can Store; refer to rule XXX
      8.2.2.4. Purchaser May Not Buy a Resource if None are in the Resource Track
  8.3. Repeat Rule 8.1 Until All Players are Finished
9. Build
  9.1. Turn Order From Last to First (Last Slot to Slot 1); Current Player is the Builder
  9.2. Builder Chooses:
    9.2.1. Pass, Skip to Rule 9.4
    9.2.2. Build Cities
      9.2.2.1. Player Pays the Following Cost
        9.2.2.1.1. Cheapest Cost to Add the City to the Player's Network of Cities
          9.2.2.1.1.1. Except, if the Player Owns no Cities, Then the Cost is 0
          9.2.2.1.1.2. Otherwise, a Graph Search Will Determine the Cost For the Builder
        9.2.2.1.2. Plus, the lowest Fee of X based on:
          9.2.2.1.2.1. 10, if City Empty
          9.2.2.1.2.2. 15, if One Other Player
          9.2.2.1.2.3. 20, if Two Other Players
      9.2.2.2. Except, the Player May Not Build on a City if Three Other Players Built Previously
      9.2.2.3. Except, the Player May Not Build on a City if the Players in a City Would Exceed the Current Step
      9.2.2.4. Total Cost to Build May Not Exceed the Builder's Owned Money
      9.2.3.5. Repeat Rule 9.2.2  Until No More Cities are Wanted
  9.3. If A Player Has More Cities Than the Cost Of a Plant In the Market, Remove the Plant From the Game And Replace
  9.4. Repeat Rule 9.1 Until All Builders are Done
  9.5. Update the Track For The Number of Cities Owned
10. Bureaucracy
  10.1. No Turn Order Enforced; The Player Making Choices Below is the Operator
  10.2. Operator Chooses:
    10.2.1. Pass, Skip to Rule 10.4
    10.2.2. Activate a Power Plant
      10.2.2.1. Except, May Not Activate if Insufficient Resources; Refer to Rule XXX
      10.2.2.2. Remove Resources Needed to Activate; Refer to Rule XXX
      10.2.2.3. Adds to the Total of Powered Cities This Round
        10.2.2.3.1. Except, the Total Can Not Exceed The Number of the Operator's Owned Cities
  10.3. Repeat Rule 10.2 Until No More Power Plants or Pass
  10.4. The Operator Gets Paid By the Rate Specified for the Total Number of Cities Powered This Round
  10.5. Repeat 10.1 Until All Players are Finished
  10.6. Replenish Resources According to Rule 4
  10.7. Remove a Power Plant in the Market
    10.7.1. If Step 3, Choose the Lowest Cost Plant
      10.7.1.1. Unless Step 3 Was First Triggered This Round During Phase 2
    10.7.2. Otherwise, Choose the Highest Cost Plant And Place Under the Draw Deck
11. Triggering And Effects of Steps
  11.1. Step 2
    11.1.1. Triggered Just Before Bureaucracy Phase, after Build Phase
    11.1.2. Two Players May Now Build in the Same City
    11.1.3. Remove and Replace the Lowest Cost Power Plant
  11.2. Step 3
    11.2.1. If Revealed During Auction Phase
      11.2.1.1. Immediately Shuffle Draw Stack
      11.2.1.2. After the End of the Auction Phase, remove the Step 3 Card and the Lowest Cost Power Plant in the Market
      11.2.1.3. Step 3 Starts in the Build Phase
    11.2.2. If Revealed During Build Phase
      11.2.2.1. Remove the Step 3 Card and the Lowest Cost Power Plant in the Market
      11.2.2.2. Shuffle the Draw Stack
      11.2.2.3. Step 3 Starts in the Bureaucracy Phase
    11.2.3. If Revealed During Bureaucracy Phase
      11.2.3.1. Remove the Step 3 Card and the Lowest Cost Power Plant in the Market
      11.2.3.2. Shuffle the Draw Stack
      11.2.3.3. Resupply at Step 2 Rates
      11.2.3.4. Step 3 Starts in the Auction Phase
    11.2.4. Only 6 Power Plants are in the Market at a Time
    11.2.5. Three Players May Now Build in the Same City
    11.2.6. Remove from the Game the Lowest Cost Power Plant From the Market in Subsequent Rounds of the Game
12. Check for Game End
  12.1. If the Number of Owned Cities of Any Player Equals or is Greater Than Specified in Rule 2.3
    12.1.1. Whomever Can Supply the Most Number of Cities With Power Wins; Refer to Rule 10.2 without Passing
    12.1.2. If a Tie, Whichever Player with the Most Cities Wins
    12.1.3. If Still a Tie, The Player That Meets 12.1.1 and 12.1.2 and has the Most Cities Owned Wins
    12.1.4. The Game is Over, and No More Actions may be Performed
13. Miscellaneous
  13.1. Players May Move Resources Between Power Plants at any Time; Refer to Rule XXX for Restrictions
